# Ethics & Responsible Use of AI Text Humanizers

## Purpose

This document outlines ethical guidelines for using AI text humanization tools listed in this repository.

## ✅ Legitimate Uses

- **Writing Enhancement:** Improving clarity, flow, and readability of drafts
- **Accessibility:** Helping non-native English speakers communicate more effectively
- **Learning:** Understanding differences between AI and human writing patterns
- **Content Creation:** Streamlining content production workflows with human oversight
- **Therapeutic/Assistive:** Supporting individuals with writing difficulties or disabilities

## ❌ Unethical Uses

- **Academic Dishonesty:** Submitting AI-humanized work as original without disclosure
- **Plagiarism:** Using humanizers to disguise copied content
- **Deception:** Misrepresenting AI-generated content as human-authored where prohibited
- **Misinformation:** Creating deceptive content designed to evade detection
- **Circumventing Safeguards:** Bypassing institutional or platform AI detection policies

## Recommendations

1. **Always disclose** AI assistance in academic and professional contexts
2. **Follow institutional policies** regarding AI tool usage
3. **Use as a writing aid**, not a replacement for original thinking
4. **Respect terms of service** of detection tools and academic platforms
5. **Support transparency** in AI-generated content

## Institutional Guidelines

Different institutions have varying policies on AI tool usage:

- Check your **university's academic integrity policy**
- Review your **workplace's AI usage guidelines**
- Follow **journal/publisher requirements** for AI tool disclosure
- Respect **platform terms of service** (e.g., freelance platforms, content marketplaces)
